Founded 30 years ago to reflect the wonderful heritage and tradition of perfumery from the Sultanate of Oman, Amouage is an international luxury fragrance house that draws inspiration form cultures around the world.

Devoted to creating finely blended perfumes with the highest quality of ingredients sourced from around the world, Amouage brings artistry to perfumery with an incomparable distinction. Original and evocative, Amouage perfumes are created by Christopher Chong, Creative Director, with internationally recognized perfumers in Paris and Grasse , in the South of France.

At the heart of all Amouage creations is a signature accord that cultivates the ultimate luxury of being unique and distinctive.

Dalí Haute Parfumerie draws its inspiration from the permanent exhibition, “Dalí- Jewels,” to which the Fundació Gala-Salvador Dalí has dedicated a museum next to the Dalí Theatre-Museum in Figueres. Borrowing several emblems from the Masterʼs iconography, Dalí Haute Parfumerie follows one of his ambitions: to create jewelry pieces that please the eye, while raising the spirits and touching the heart.

Like a jewelry piece by Dalí, the fragrance takes on a completely new dimension when it touches the skin. It enhances the wearer by giving her artistic control of her life. The exceptional bottles, designed with the same eye for luxury and excellence, showcase the divine senses. Figurative and expressive, they feature the quintessence of the artistʼs spirit: a skillfully and freely interpreted tribute to an original piece of art. Becoming jewels in themselves, the bottles hint at the treasures they hold within and weave a bond between two forms of language: that of the perfumer and that of the artist, uniting smell, touch and sight, the senses of sensuality.

Nose Alberto Morillas selected the rarest raw materials in Perfumery, like exceptional gems, to express precious scents reflecting the soul of Salvador Dali and his sumptuous and exceptional goldsmith work from the mid-20th century.

Five new fragrances continue the story of the great surrealist, inspired by his jewellery line. For the first five eau de parfum symbols were chosen which Dali appreciated and widely used: an eye (Regard Scintillant de Mille Beautes), butterfly (Voyage Onirique du Papillon de Vie), goblet (Calice de la seduction), hand (Melodie du Cygne de la Main) and the clock (Fluidite du temps Imaginaire).
